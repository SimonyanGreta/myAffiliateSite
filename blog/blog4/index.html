Coffee Processing Methods

Wet Process Washed

Coffee beans are fermented and washed to remove the mucilage, resulting in clean and bright flavors.

Dry Process Natural

Coffee cherries are dried whole, imparting fruity and sweet notes into the beans.

Honey Process

A balance between washed and natural: mucilage remains during drying, creating complex flavors.
